Rankings

Explore rankings tables that show how all 94 universities compare for the following 3 social mobility metrics:

  • Equal access
    This metric compares each university's student intake against the socio-economic makeup of all 18 to 19 year olds in England. It shows how representative each university’s student body is of the general population, regardless of their academic grades.
  • Social mobility coefficient
    This metric compares the students who enrol at each university against the pool of 18 to 19 year olds in England who achieved the required grades for that university.
  • Social mobility coefficient (adjusted for location)
    This adjusts the social mobility coefficient to account for geography by giving more importance to qualified students who live close to the university.
