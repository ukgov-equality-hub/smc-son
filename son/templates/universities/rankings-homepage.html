  • Equal access
    This metric assesses how effectively a university recruits students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds compared to the demographic makeup of its surrounding area. A higher 'Equal access' score indicates better recruitment of disadvantaged students.
  • Social mobility coefficient
    This coefficient measures the impact of socioeconomic background on student outcomes (e.g. degree attainment, employment). It accounts for factors like prior academic achievement and chosen field of study. A higher coefficient suggests that a university is more successful at mitigating the disadvantages faced by students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds.
  • Social mobility coefficient (location weighting)
    Similar to the Social mobility coefficient, but this version incorporates a weighting based on the student's geographical location. This provides a more nuanced view by considering local socioeconomic factors that might influence student outcomes, offering a fairer comparison between institutions in different regions.
