Rankings

Understanding the metrics presented in this tool is key to gaining insights into social mobility at universities. Here's a brief guide:

  • Equal access
    This metric compares a university's actual student intake against the socio-economic makeup of all young people in England. It provides a baseline showing how representative the student body is of the general population, regardless of their academic grades.
  • Social mobility coefficient
    This metric compares the students who actually enrol against the specific pool of young people across the country who achieved the required grades for that university. A positive score means the university successfully recruits a more disadvantaged cohort than its available qualified pool, while a negative score suggests there is room for improvement.
  • Social mobility coefficient location-adjusted
    This adjusts the previous score to account for geography by placing higher importance on qualified students who live close to the university campus. This provides a more nuanced view by considering local socioeconomic factors that might influence student outcomes, offering a fairer comparison between institutions in different regions.
