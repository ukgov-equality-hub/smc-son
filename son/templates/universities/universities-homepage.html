University benchmarks for better social mobility

This is a tool developed by the Social Mobility Commission to help students, parents, and universities and policy makers to explore detailed benchmarks on university access and social mobility. It is a diagnostic tool for understanding how universities support disadvantaged students.

You can find the following sections:

  • Benchmarks by university
    Dive into data for individual universities. View detailed cumulative percentage charts and see how selected universities rank on key social mobility metrics.
  • Overall rankings
    Explore how to interpret each metric – equal access, social mobility coefficient, and social mobility coefficient location adjusted – and how all universities compare across them.
  • About the data
    Find important information regarding the methodology, data sources, and limitations of the data.

This analysis tracks real students' educational journeys using official government records, following them from their GCSEs in 2015 to 2016 to their university enrolment at age 18 or 19 (usually enrolments in 2018 to 2020). We deliberately used this pre-pandemic data to provide a clear, fair, and stable reference point for university admissions before the disruptions caused by COVID-19.

The specific datasets used are: National Pupil Database (NPD) from DfE, Higher Education Statistics Agency (HESA) student record, Get Information About Schools (GIAS) service from DfE and National Statistics Postcode Lookup provided by the Office for National Statistics (ONS).
