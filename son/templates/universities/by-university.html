Things you need to know

Socio-economic status ‘deciles’ are calculated by grouping students into 10 equal groups, from decile 1 (the most socio-economically disadvantaged students) to decile 10 (the most advantaged).

The socio-economic status of students is based on:

  • whether they received free school meal
  • whether they attended a state or independent school
  • the wealth of their local neighbourhood

To estimate the percentage of students who met each university’s academic requirements, we use the actual A-level and BTEC grades of the students who successfully enrolled, not the advertised entry guidelines. We excluded the lowest 10% of these grades to account for special cases.

The location adjustment gives more importance to qualified students who both:

  • meet the university’s entry requirements
  • live close to the university

This reflects the reality that many disadvantaged students prefer to study locally.

You should use:

  • the social mobility coefficient ranking (adjusted for location) for universities that recruit locally
  • the non-adjusted view for universities that recruit nationally

Data sources

This analysis tracks real students' educational journeys using official government records. The data follows students from their GCSEs in the 2015 to 2016 academic year, to their university enrolment at age 18 or 19 (between 2018 and 2020). We deliberately used data from before the COVID-19 pandemic to provide a clear, fair, and stable reference point before the disruptions caused by the pandemic.

The specific datasets used are:

  • National Pupil Database (NPD) from the Department for Education (DfE)
  • student record data from the Higher Education Statistics Agency (HESA)
  • Get Information About Schools (GIAS) service from DfE
  • National Statistics Postcode Lookup from the Office for National Statistics (ONS)

See About the data for more information about the data and methodology. You can also read the full report.
